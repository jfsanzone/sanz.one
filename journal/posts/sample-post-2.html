AI Won't Replace Designers. Taste Will.

January 29, 2026

Here's what happened when generative AI tools became good enough to produce passable UI in seconds: everyone got access to the same median. Midjourney renders the same lush gradients for every prompt. ChatGPT scaffolds the same card-based layouts for every brief. The tools are extraordinary, and they've made the average dramatically better. But they've also made the average dramatically more common.

The designers who thrive in this landscape aren't the ones who prompt fastest. They're the ones who can look at four AI-generated options and know, immediately, why three of them are wrong, and articulate exactly what's missing from the fourth. That instinct isn't prompt engineering. It's taste. It's the accumulated residue of a thousand deliberate decisions about type, space, rhythm, and restraint. You can't generate taste. You develop it, slowly, by caring about things most people don't notice.

I've watched junior designers produce stunning mockups in minutes with generative tools, then struggle to explain why the layout breaks on mobile or why the hierarchy falls apart when real content replaces placeholder text. The tool did the rendering. But rendering was never the hard part. The hard part is knowing what to render, and more importantly, what to leave out. AI is a powerful instrument. But an instrument without judgment is just noise.
