I'm in the Meeting, I Promise

March 6, 2026

I'm the quiet one in most meetings. Not checked out. Not multitasking. The opposite, actually.

My brain doesn't filter things the way most people's do. Everything registers. All of it, all at once. Not just the meeting but everything around it. It doesn't stop. Someone shifts in their chair when a deadline gets mentioned and I clock it. Someone's nodding along saying "yeah that makes sense" but their face decided it didn't about three seconds ago. I'm tracking the actual conversation, the one happening underneath the one everyone's performing. The micro-expressions, the tone shifts, the way someone picks their words more carefully when they're not bought in. That's where the real signal is. Not in whoever's talking the loudest.

It doesn't shut off. At home, my girlfriend will hit me with "you're not listening" while I'm on my laptop. I could give her the gist. But I'll repeat back the last few sentences in her exact tone of voice, word for word, just for funzies. She's never amused. You learn to keep most of it to yourself because the alternative is people thinking you're a creep.

The other thing is, half the time I already know who's going to say what needs to be said. So I wait. And they do. And then three more people say the same thing slightly different. Not because it needed to be said again, but because nobody wants to be the one who didn't say anything. Except me, apparently.

I know it's not how the game is played. Maybe I'm just wired to play it differently. I could chime in with the classic "just echoing so and so," or throw in a "yeah I was going to say the same thing," and it'd be fine.

But fine was never really the point.
