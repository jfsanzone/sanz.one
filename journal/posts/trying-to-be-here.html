Trying to Be Here

March 15, 2026

I had a good weekend. That shouldn't feel like news, but it does.

My girlfriend and I watched some shows. Went to a botanical garden. Ran a few errands. Got pizza at a little spot I've been meaning to try. None of it was productive. None of it moved a project forward. And I think that's the point.

Most weekends I'm physically off but mentally still at work. The laptop might be closed but my brain isn't. I'm thinking about what we need to ship on Monday, what I should be building on the side, what I'm falling behind on. Even when I'm scrolling my phone it's not really relaxing. It's just a different kind of busy. TV time usually means I'm half watching and half on my laptop or phone. That's the default.

This weekend I tried to just be in it. Not perfectly. I caught myself reaching for my phone a few times. Sitting at the botanical garden thinking about a layout I want to redesign. Sitting in a pizza spot and opening Slack out of reflex. But I noticed it. And I put the phone back in my pocket, which is more than I usually do.

The hard part isn't doing things. It's doing things without the voice in the back of your head telling you this doesn't count. That sitting on the couch watching a show is time you could be spending getting ahead. That rest is something you earn and you haven't earned it yet.

I think the real thing I'm afraid of is falling behind. Not behind anyone specific. Just behind some version of myself that's always doing more. That guy doesn't take weekends off. He doesn't wander around a garden looking at plants. He's always on.

But that guy's also not much fun to be around. The APA ran a study that said 58% of people are actually more productive after taking real time off. Which means the always-on version of me might not even be the more effective one.

I don't have a system for this. No screen time limits or morning routines or weekend rituals. I just had a weekend where I tried to be where I was. Some of it I had to force. Some of it just happened. And now it's Sunday night and I feel like I actually had two days off for once.

That's it. That's the whole thing. I'm trying to be here.
